The challenge

The traditional travel enquiry funnel leaks.

Trip A Deal's website and email programmes generate significant inbound demand. But the conversion layer — turning interest into a conversation — relies on slow, manual follow-up that lets warm leads go cold.

Leads sit in a queue for hours

Holiday shoppers compare 3–4 providers simultaneously. A 90-minute response window is the difference between a booking and a lost customer.

Consultants are capacity constrained

Your best consultants only manually work the highest-intent enquiries. Medium and low-intent leads — who just need a nudge — fall through entirely.

Nurture emails are one-size-fits-all

Rules-based drip sequences send the same content to a cruiser and a gap-year backpacker. Personalisation at scale is impossible without AI.

Website traffic doesn't become pipeline

Visitors browse tours, use the search filters, view departure dates — and leave. There is no real-time layer that converts that intent into a consultant conversation.

01
Website AI Agent · Piper on tripadeal.com.au

Capture the midnight tour browser before they leave.

AI website agent · routes enquiries into Salesforce CRM in real time

What it does

Piper monitors visitor behaviour on Trip A Deal's website — pages viewed, tours searched, departure filters used, time on site, return visits. When a visitor shows buying intent, Piper engages in chat or voice, qualifies them with scripted questions, and books a call with the right travel consultant's calendar.

Visitors who leave without booking get an immediate personalised follow-up email. Every conversation is logged to Salesforce with full context. The consultant receives a warm, pre-qualified lead — not a cold form fill.

Why it matters

Trip A Deal has strong organic and paid traffic. The drop-off is on the website, at night, on weekends. A customer comparing Fiji packages at 10pm will not call a consultant in the morning. They'll book with whoever engaged them in the moment.

  • Works 24/7 — no consultants rostered on
  • Qualifies against Trip A Deal's own criteria (destination, budget, travel dates, group size)
  • Books directly into consultant calendars
  • All conversations logged to Salesforce automatically
  • Full context shared with consultant before the call

02
AI Lead Nurture · Piper in the inbox

Follow up every lead — every time, instantly.

AI email nurture agent · native inside Salesforce · works every lead regardless of intent level

What it does

Piper runs automated, 1:1 personalised email follow-up for every lead in Salesforce — whether they downloaded a brochure, registered for a webinar, attended a travel expo, or enquired about a specific departure. It sends, watches for replies, answers questions from an approved knowledge library, and books a call when the lead is ready.

Your consultants only get involved when there's a conversation worth having. Every interaction is logged in Salesforce automatically.

Why it matters

A consultant receives 20+ leads on Monday. By Friday they've spoken to four. Sixteen go cold — three of whom were ready to book, they just needed a timely nudge and an answer to one question about inclusions.

  • Instant first-touch — within minutes of lead creation
  • Personalised by destination, intent, and engagement history
  • Answers FAQs (inclusions, visa, group sizes, departure dates)
  • Re-engages event and expo leads with post-event content
  • Escalates to consultant the moment a meeting is requested

Provisioning requirements

Qualified is a buy-and-configure product. The items below are what Trip A Deal needs to have in place — or complete — before or during the 30-day implementation. Most are low effort; they're listed in likely sequence.

1

Salesforce CRM access

Qualified connects natively to Salesforce. You'll need an active Salesforce org with Leads or Contacts objects in use. An API user account with appropriate permissions is required during setup. If Trip A Deal uses a custom CRM or HubSpot, a custom integration path is available but adds ~2 weeks.

2

Website JavaScript snippet

A single JS tag is added to tripadeal.com.au (similar to Google Analytics). This powers the website agent (Solution 01 and 03). Requires access to the site's tag management system (Google Tag Manager preferred) or developer deployment. No CMS changes needed.

3

Sending domain authentication

For Piper to send emails on behalf of Trip A Deal (Solution 02), a subdomain needs to be configured (e.g. piper.tripadeal.com.au) with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. This is a one-time DNS change, typically completed by your IT team in under an hour.

4

Knowledge base content

Piper answers questions from an approved knowledge library. You provide the source content — FAQs, product fact sheets, inclusion lists, visa information, departure details — and the Qualified team ingests and structures it during onboarding. No technical work required from Trip A Deal.

5

Consultant calendar integration

For meeting booking to work, consultant calendars need to be connected. Qualified integrates with Google Calendar and Microsoft 365 / Outlook natively. Each consultant connects their own calendar via OAuth — takes under 5 minutes per person. Routing rules (destination specialist, availability) are configured in Qualified's Agent Studio.

6

Brand voice and guardrails

Piper operates within boundaries you define — tone, products in scope, what she will and won't discuss, escalation rules. The Qualified onboarding team runs a 2-session configuration workshop (~3hrs total) to set this up. No technical knowledge required from Trip A Deal.

Note on Salesforce as a prerequisite

Qualified is built natively on Salesforce and is most powerful when Salesforce CRM is the system of record for leads and customers. If Trip A Deal does not currently use Salesforce, a lightweight Salesforce Sales Cloud org can be provisioned specifically to support Qualified, without replacing any existing CRM. The Salesforce team can scope this as a parallel workstream.
